DAY 3

1. NAVIGATION

What is meant by Navigation?

Navigation is about how easy it is for people to take action and move around the website.

How to achieve good Navigation?

  • Have clear and concise menu links

  • Utilize CTA buttons and other clickable buttons

  • Emphasis the 3 click rule in your design and development

    • This is so that visitors can get the required information within 3 clicks

2. ICONS

What are Icons and their purpose?

  • They communicate the core idea and intent of a product or action.

  • They bring a lot of nice benefits to user interfaces, such as saving screen space and enhancing aesthetic appeal.

There are three types of icons:

  • Universal

  • Conflicting

  • Unique icons

Universal Icons

  • These are icons that are recognizable.

  • Their representation is usually understood at first glance.

  • Used widespread across different platforms

Example

  • The shopping cart icon on eCommerce sites

  • Magnifying glass for Search Bars

There is only one problem: Universal icons are rare.

Beyond the examples cited above, most icons are ambiguous.

They can have different meanings depending on the interface.

Conflicting Icons

Trouble comes when common icons are implemented with contradictory meanings.

Depending on which platform you see them, the functionality associated with them usually varies. They sometimes seem to do the same things.

Conflicting icons are hard to interpret as they can represent different things for different users

Unique Icons

One-of-a-kind or abstract icons that are used for representation.

Be careful with using unique icons they are not always clear to the user at first glance.
